Male Reproductive System

Main Structures :

  • Testes: Produce sperm and testosterone (located in scrotum)
  • Scrotum: Keeps testes cooler than body temperature
  • Sperm duct: Carries sperm from testes to urethra
  • Prostate gland: Adds fluid to sperm to make semen
  • Penis: Delivers semen into vagina during sex
  • Urethra: Tube carrying semen or urine

Female Reproductive System

Main Structures :

  • Ovaries: Produce eggs and hormones (estrogen, progesterone)
  • Oviducts: Tubes where fertilization occurs (also called fallopian tubes)
  • Uterus: Where embryo develops (womb)
  • Cervix: Entrance to uterus
  • Vagina: Receives penis during sex, birth canal

Additional Info:

Females are born with all their eggs already formed. Each month, one egg matures and is released during ovulation.

Fertilization & Pregnancy

Fertilization Process :

  1. Sperm deposited in vagina during sexual intercourse
  2. Sperm swim through cervix and uterus to oviduct
  3. One sperm penetrates the egg's membrane (fertilization)
  4. Egg forms a barrier to prevent other sperm entering
  5. Fertilized egg (zygote) divides by mitosis as it moves to uterus
  6. Embryo implants in uterus lining (implantation)

Additional info:

The placenta allows exchange of oxygen, nutrients and waste between mother and fetus without their blood mixing. The umbilical cord connects fetus to placenta.
